Lumen IV Aeroshell

Structural Subsystem Lead · Adelaide University Solar Racing · 2026–present

Leading the aeroshell design for the Lumen IV solar vehicle as part of my final year project, building the exterior geometry in Autodesk Alias and iterating the aerodynamic form through CFD simulation in ANSYS Fluent. Lumen IV is a much more ambitious design than the previous vehicle, it has three wheels for lower mass and rolling resistance. The main body is tilted to reduce frontal area, improve the angle of solar incidence and improve non-driver side vision. It has also been designed with mechanical packaging in mind by following a process of parallel design with mechanical subsystems.

SOLAR VEHICLE · FEA · MECHANISM DESIGN

Lumen III

Team Member · Adelaide University Solar Racing · 2024–2025

Designed, built and raced Lumen III in collaboration with a large team. Lumen III was a huge multi-disciplinary project, ranging from composites production, to electronic systems and race strategy. I was a race participant and worked as a member of the pit crew in Darwin where i spent two weeks preparing the car for operation, making repairs, fixing issues as we encountered them during testing, and living and working as a team. Unfortunately we were unable to complete the race due to an unexpected electrical component failure during the race (of which there was no replacement in Central Australia) but we were able to drive the vehicle over 1000 km and cross the finish line in Adelaide. I designed and built the movable wheel fairings mechanism. Which was a hammer and pivot system which opened the side fairng panels allowing the vehicle to meet the required turning circle as per the regulations. I also fabricated a large amount of the composite body as an intern with the team where we were hosted at Etamax Engineering and worked in their composites workshop under their supervision for 6 months.

Design · Aerodynamics

Streamlined Drone

Personal Project · 2026

Designed drone body, powertrain and internal structure of streamlined FPV drone. Goal of the project is to produce a concept which can break the current world record of 657.6 km/h for a quadcopter type drone. A paremtric analysis was done in python comparing thrust balance and pitching angle to return viable configurations. To get accurate parameters and inputs into the model ANSYS Fluent was utilised to calculate a drag area and the centre of pressure, the calculated drag area in early analysis was found to be 0.00078 m², equating to 16.19 N of drag at Mach 0.54. The difference between this personal project and my work with AU Solar Racing is the greater velocity placing the flow well into the compressable regime, therefore a compressible solver was utilised to return results making this project a more difficult aerodynamic simulation than the solar car.
